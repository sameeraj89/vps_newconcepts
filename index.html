VPS Two-Campus Strategy · April 2026

Two large centres. One system. Nothing like it in Kochi.

Transform VPS Lakeshore into a purely referral-driven specialist hospital — tertiary and quaternary cases only, anchoring Kerala's most complex care: transplant, cardiac surgery, CAR-T, and neurosurgery. Build VPS Kakkanad as a 200-bed multispecialty hospital on the SmartCity land parcel — high-quality, accessible, community-facing, and capital-efficient. It captures everyday volume and feeds complex cases upward, while limiting capex and reducing early-stage losses. Together they serve Kochi's fastest-growing urban population — young IT professionals, NRI families, and the 140,000-strong Infopark–SmartCity workforce — through a managed referral loop no competitor in Kerala can replicate.

India's oncology market is growing at 19.8% CAGR ($3.3B → $7.8B by 2030), and Delhi has among the highest cancer incidence rates for men in the country. Yet Delhi's cancer care is fragmented across multispecialty hospitals — RGCI, Fortis, Max, and Medanta all treat cancer as one department among thirty. No private hospital in North India integrates haem-onc, BMT, CAR-T, rare disease, genomics, and clinical trials under one purpose-built roof.

The rare disease gap is even starker. An estimated 80–100 million Indians have rare diseases, but there are only 11 government-designated Centres of Excellence nationally — four in Delhi, all capacity-constrained with 3–6 month waitlists. There is no private-sector rare disease hospital in North India. The government has signalled intent: NFRD funding is ₹974 Cr for FY24–26, support raised to ₹50 lakh per patient, orphan drug approvals fast-tracked.

BMT capacity in NCR is severely constrained — BLK, Fortis, and Max have long waitlists. India's BMT market ($783M → $1.49B by 2032) is the fastest growing in APAC, and CAR-T therapy (ImmunoACT NexCAR19) is now approved domestically at ~₹30L vs $400K in the US. A purpose-built transplant centre with 10+ HEPA rooms absorbs this unmet demand from day one.

Delhi today
Cancer care is a department, not a mission. Rare disease barely exists. Genomic profiling outsourced. BMT waitlists run months. Clinical trials are bolt-on. Oncologists share OTs and ICU beds with ortho and cardiac.

VPS Lakeshore's 2,000+ transplant track record. Adjacent to IIT Delhi and JNU for academic partnerships.

⑤ Global benchmarks & collaboration targets — the company VPS Qutub keeps

VPS Qutub should not be designed to compete with Max or Medanta. It should be designed to belong in the same conversation as the world's best integrated cancer and rare disease centres. These institutions share a common model: research and clinical care under one roof, universal genomic profiling, structured rare disease pipelines, and deep pharma partnerships. Each offers a design principle VPS can adopt.

Formal collaboration agreements with 2–3 of these centres would transform VPS Delhi's talent proposition — senior Indian clinicians who currently leave for Western institutions would have a reason to stay. International academic affiliations attract trial sponsors, publish-or-perish physicians, and patient referrals from across South Asia and the Gulf.

Karolinska University Hospital
Stockholm · 730 beds · 36 OTs · Precision Medicine Centre
The Genomic Medicine Center Karolinska (GMCK) has sequenced 15,000+ patient genomes with 23% diagnostic yield for rare diseases. Tripartite model: research institute + hospital + SciLifeLab genomic infrastructure as one unit. Design principle: embed genomics into routine diagnostics, not as an add-on service.
MD Anderson Cancer Center
Houston · 760 beds · #1 US cancer centre · IMPACT programme
First cross-tumour precision medicine programme (IMPACT, 2007) proved molecularly matched therapy improves survival. McCombs Institute houses 7 translational research centres. Runs more NCI-funded cancer trials than any US centre. Design principle: molecular tumour board where every patient's tumour is profiled and matched to trials.
Memorial Sloan Kettering
New York · 4,700 daily outpatients · MSK-IMPACT (468 genes)
MSK-IMPACT is FDA-authorised and run on every solid tumour patient. Results auto-feed into the EHR and match patients to 300+ early-phase trials. The Koch Center (2020): 25 floors, 231 exam rooms, 110 private infusion bays. Design principle: universal tumour profiling is operationally feasible and drives trial enrolment from day one.
GOSH Zayed Centre for Rare Disease
London · 339 beds · 13,000 sqm · 50,000 genomic reports/yr
Purpose-built rare disease research facility with 7 specialised clean-room labs for cell and gene therapy manufacturing. Europe's largest dedicated gene therapy manufacturing site co-located with a children's hospital. Design principle: co-locate rare disease care with gene therapy manufacturing and genomic diagnostics under one roof.
Netherlands Cancer Institute (NKI-AvL)
Amsterdam · 180 beds · Research + clinical fully integrated
The only dedicated cancer research hospital in the Netherlands. Every clinical floor has an adjacent research lab. Bold angular architecture signals institutional purpose. Runs 400+ clinical trials at any time. Design principle: architecture communicates mission. NKI is the closest physical model for VPS Qutub at 180 vs 150 beds.
Mayo Clinic — Center for Individualized Medicine
Rochester · 2,059 beds · 5 translational programmes
CIM is organised into programmes (Biomarker Discovery, Clinomics, Epigenomics, Microbiome, Pharmacogenomics) that overlay across all clinical departments — not a siloed precision medicine department. Design principle: programme-based precision medicine overlay is ideal for a 150-bed hospital where standalone departments are too costly.

⑧ Why VPS wins against Max and Fortis

Max and Fortis are large multispecialties where oncology competes with 30 other departments for OT time, ICU beds, and capital. Research is a bolt-on. Rare disease barely registers. Cancer patients feel like a number. VPS Qutub inverts this entirely — every floor, every bed, every hire is about complex care. The niche focus avoids the crowded positioning of general hospitals while creating structural moats:

Genomics monopoly — Delhi's only NABL clinical genomics lab makes every NCR hospital a referrer
BMT first-mover — 10+ HEPA rooms and CAR-T capability creates a 3–5 year head start in a constrained market
Rare disease data — India's first patient registry and database. Pharma pays for access, patients self-refer nationally
International pipeline — Burjeel's Gulf network is structural. Middle East oncology patients currently go to Singapore or Turkey. India is the cost-quality play
Doctor loyalty — Equity, research freedom, and a Burjeel international network are switching costs no Delhi chain offers

⑩ Pharma partnerships, clinical trials & rare disease ecosystem

Delhi is India's biggest pharma market and clinical trial hub. With 1,988 cancer trials on CTRI (456 in 2022 alone), India hosts ~20% of global trials at roughly half the cost. 50% of trials are pharma-sponsored. Delhi NCR is the single largest cluster for cancer trials — AIIMS, RGCI, Fortis, and Max are active sites — but none is a dedicated rare disease trial site.

India's oncology pharma market is ₹3.6B (2022) growing to ₹10.6B by 2030. VPS Delhi should position as a clinical trials site + rare disease treatment centre + drug access programme partner. A centre running 8–10 concurrent trials generates ₹2–4 Cr/year in trial income alone, plus pharmacy volume uplift and pharma-funded infrastructure.

Four government rare disease CoEs exist in Delhi (AIIMS, MAMC/Lok Nayar, Lady Hardinge, Army Hospital R&R) — all capacity-constrained with 3–6 month waits. There is no private-sector rare disease CoE in Delhi NCR. VPS would be the first.

⑫ Why Delhi, why Qutub — the brand-building case
Delhi is not just a hospital — it is VPS's national identity inflection point.

VPS Lakeshore today is a Kerala brand — respected regionally, unknown nationally. Every major Indian hospital chain that achieved national scale — Apollo, Fortis, Max, Medanta — did so by establishing a flagship in Delhi. Delhi is where India's healthcare reputation is made: it is where AIIMS sits, where medical tourism decisions are influenced, where corporate payors make empanelment decisions, and where referring doctors from across North India send their complex cases.

A purpose-built cancer and rare disease centre in the Qutub Institutional Area — in the heart of South Delhi's academic-medical corridor — announces VPS as a national complex care and oncology player, not just a Kerala transplant hospital. This is the single highest-leverage brand investment VPS can make.

Why a niche, differentiated product wins

Cancer + rare disease + BMT = defensible niche: This combination doesn't exist anywhere in India. RGCI does cancer but not rare disease. Fortis does BMT but not genomics. No one does all three at CoE level

Clinical trials revenue: A purpose-built facility with GCP-compliant infrastructure attracts global pharma. Trial revenue per patient is 3–5× standard care — and pharma companies pay for infrastructure
🔧
Operational depth — what to build in from day one
Capabilities that differentiate operationally, not just clinically
Tumour board as a service: Weekly virtual multi-disciplinary tumour boards connecting VPS Delhi, VPS Kochi, and Burjeel UAE — creating India's first cross-geography oncology network
Clinical decision support AI: Embed AI-driven treatment pathway recommendation (Watson for Oncology model) from the start — positions VPS as technology-forward, attracts younger oncologists
Patient navigator programme: Dedicated coordinators for every cancer and rare disease patient — from diagnosis through treatment to survivorship. This is the HCG/Narayana model that drives NPS above 85
Integrated genomics workflow: Every cancer patient gets molecular profiling at admission. Results feed into treatment selection. This is standard in the US/UK but rare in India — VPS does it from day one
Outcomes registry: Publish clinical outcomes data openly — survival rates, complication rates, patient experience scores. No Indian private hospital does this consistently. Radical transparency becomes the brand

Future-readiness — built for the next decade
Infrastructure and capability investments that compound over time
CAR-T manufacturing readiness: India approved its first CAR-T (NexCAR19) in 2024. The next wave is autologous CAR-T at point of care. Build the clean room and cell processing lab into the G+8 design now — retrofit later costs 3× more

Liquid biopsy & early detection: Cancer screening is shifting from imaging to blood-based biomarkers. Build the lab infrastructure for ctDNA analysis, methylation panels, and multi-cancer early detection tests — this becomes a high-margin revenue stream
Health data platform: With genomics, outcomes, and patient data from day one, VPS builds India's richest cancer and rare disease dataset. This has immense value for pharma partnerships, AI model training, and precision medicine research — a long-term strategic asset
