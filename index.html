Strategic Rationale
Why Kakkanad?
Kakkanad is Kochi's IT hub — home to Infopark and SmartCity — with a young professional demographic that represents the future patient base for VPS Lakeshore. The area is experiencing rapid residential and commercial growth, making it the fastest-growing local catchment zone. Securing land now while prices are favourable creates a long-term strategic asset. A medical city development here would dramatically expand VPS's footprint in the eastern Ernakulam corridor where competitors currently lead.
Competitive Landscape
AIMS Kochi (1,300 beds) dominates this zone with 4% patient share. Aster Medcity (862 beds) holds 3%. Rajagiri Hospital gains via proximity. VPS currently captures only 2% of Kakkanad patients — the lowest among major Kochi hospitals. Recommended strategies: corporate health tie-ups with Infopark companies, executive health check-up packages for IT professionals, ortho & sports medicine marketing, and a shuttle service from Kakkanad to VPS Lakeshore.

Strategic Context
This centre is the centrepiece of the VPS Healthcare India three-year transformation plan. Delhi provides access to a national patient pool including CGHS beneficiaries, NRI stopovers from the Gulf corridor, and the north Indian oncology referral network. The vision is to become India's leading integrated network for Complex Cancer Care & Rare Disease Management. The Qutub Institutional Area location adjacent to IIT Delhi reinforces the academic and research positioning. The three-year financial projections for VPS overall show revenue growing from INR 500 Cr to INR 780 Cr (16% CAGR) with EBITDA margins expanding from 19% to 24%.
Recommendation: Do Not Proceed With This Deal
The Tirupati oncology partnership presents a high-risk, low-strategic-fit proposition for VPS Healthcare. The projected IRR of 16–17% (below the 20% minimum hurdle), combined with an unproven market for high-end complex oncology care in Tirupati and structural imbalances in the deal, does not justify committing INR 111 Cr of capital or senior management bandwidth.

Better Alternatives for This Capital
If deploying capital to build an oncology brand remains a priority — there are better options:
• Invest the INR 111 Cr into expanding clinical services capacity at VPS Lakeshore itself — where the brand, patient base, and operational platform already exist.
• Evaluate other opportunities in the Kerala market: deeper market, stronger talent pool, growing medical tourism, with specific oncology focus aligning to brand positioning.
• If the G2 relationship is worth preserving, renegotiate with fairer revenue split, volume guarantees from G2, and exit rights.

Project Background
Indorama Institute of Medical Sciences (IIMS)
A corporate business house (Indorama Group) with ancestral roots from Kota plans to develop a world-class cancer institute as a non-profit organization, providing comprehensive cancer care — from preventive to curative — to the entire Hadoti region at an affordable cost. The project concept is built around a "Social Business Model" focused on total care. The DPR was prepared by HospiHealth Consultants India Private Limited (Mumbai) after site visits to cancer hospitals in Jaipur, Kota, and surrounding Hadoti districts.

VPS Role — Assessment Needed
Due Diligence Required Before VPS Commitment
Per the VPS Healthcare India approach note (Feb 2026), Jaipur/Kota land parcels with the Indorama Group are in subsequent phases. VPS is to conduct independent due diligence and feasibility study for establishing a ~100 bed multispecialty/cancer centre with precision care, complex care, and rare disease unit. VPS's role could range from O&M provider to equity partner — this must be determined through the due diligence process. Key questions: market depth validation, non-profit sustainability model, VPS investment quantum, and how this fits with the overall expansion strategy.
